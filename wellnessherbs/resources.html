Responsible Suppliers

We recommend suppliers who provide herbs from growers which preserve wild places and promote organic agriculture. Suppliers should ensure plant species are harvested in a sustainable and legal manner. Suppliers should practice farming techniques which minimize ecological impact. Suppliers should encourage growers to set aside habitat for native pollinators.

Growers and suppliers should be good stewards of nature's bounty. Wild harvested products should be sustainably sourced to prevent overharvesting which can lead to plant extinction. As of 2020 there were 65 plant extinctions recorded in the North American continent. Many were due to plant habitat loss.

Suppliers should promote rigorous quality control procedures to ensure identity, purity and potency. Harvested herbs should be stored in climate-controlled facilities, out of direct sunlight, to maintain material quality.
